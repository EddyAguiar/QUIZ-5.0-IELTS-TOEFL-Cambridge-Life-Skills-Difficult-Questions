📌 Ordem das palavras em inglês / Word Order in English

In English, the basic word order is: Subject + Verb + Object + Place + Time. Em inglês, a ordem básica das palavras é: Sujeito + Verbo + Objeto + Lugar + Tempo.

S + V + O "I like pizza." "Eu gosto de pizza."
S + V + Place "I work in an office." "Eu trabalho em um escritório."
S + V + Time "I wake up at 7am." "Eu acordo às 7h."
S + V + O + Place + Time "I study English at home every day." "Eu estudo inglês em casa todos os dias."

❓ Perguntas com "How" / Questions with "How"

How + adjective? "How big is your house?" — "Qual o tamanho da sua casa?"
How + adverb? "How often do you travel?" — "Com que frequência você viaja?"
How many + countable? "How many friends do you have?" — "Quantos (contável) amigos você tem?"
How much + uncountable? "How much time do you have?" — "Quanto (incontável) tempo você tem?"
How long? "How long have you studied English?" — "Há quanto tempo você estuda inglês?"
How come? (informal) "How come you are late?" — "Por que (informal) você está atrasado?"

📌 Exemplos completos / Complete examples

Why? "Why do you like it?" — "Por que você gosta disso?"
How long? "How long have you lived here?" — "Há quanto tempo você mora aqui?"
How many? "How many people live with you?" — "Quantas pessoas moram com você?"
How much? "How much does it cost?" — "Quanto custa?"

💡 Dicas & Estruturas Gramaticais

🔹 Comparing & Contrasting

Use comparativos, superlativos, as...as, similar to, different from.

B1 "I prefer to eat in because it's much more convenient."
B2 "It's not as expensive as eating out, and the food is equally tasty."
C1 "While eating at home is undoubtedly more economical, dining out offers a far superior social experience."
C2 "Eating in is by far the most practical choice, yet the sheer variety of flavours in restaurants is virtually unparalleled."
💡 Vocabulário: much more, far less, equally, slightly, considerably, virtually, by far, nowhere near

🔸 Recent Changes (Present Perfect)

Use have/has + past participle ou used to + infinitive.

B1 "Yes, I have become much more health-conscious recently."
B2 "I used to eat a lot of junk food, but I've switched to a balanced diet."
C1 "Over the past few years, my eating habits have undergone a complete transformation."
C2 "Having been a fast-food enthusiast, I have since adopted a plant-based diet, which has had a profound impact on my wellbeing."
💡 Vocabulário: recently, lately, over the past, since, for, already, yet, just, ever, never

🔹 Past Experiences

Use past simple e past continuous.

B1 "I came here by train. There were no delays."
B2 "I was taking the train when I realised I had left my phone at home."
C1 "Had I known about the delays, I would have taken the bus instead."
C2 "Having travelled extensively by train, I was well aware of the potential disruptions, yet I opted for the railway nonetheless."
💡 Vocabulário: ago, last, yesterday, when, while, as, during, before, after, already, yet

🔸 Frequency

Use advérbios de frequência: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never.

B1 "I often take the bus. I use it on a daily basis."
B2 "I rarely drive to work; I usually cycle or take the tube."
C1 "More often than not, I find myself relying on public transport, particularly during rush hour."
C2 "In the vast majority of cases, I eschew private vehicles in favour of public transit, a practice I have maintained for over a decade."
💡 Vocabulário: always, usually, often, sometimes, occasionally, rarely, never, every day, once a week, on a daily basis, more often than not

🔹 Hypothetical (would/could)

Use would/could + infinitive ou second conditional (if + past, would + infinitive).

B1 "If I could go anywhere, I would visit Thailand."
B2 "I would definitely consider moving abroad if I were offered a good job."
C1 "Were I to have more free time, I would undoubtedly take up a new hobby like painting."
C2 "Had I the opportunity, I would seize the chance to travel the world, immersing myself in diverse cultures."
💡 Vocabulário: would, could, might, if I were, were I to, had I, suppose, imagine, in case
